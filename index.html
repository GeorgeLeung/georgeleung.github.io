About me

I am a Physics Ph.D. candidate at Emory University in Theoretical Biophysics. My research focuses on modelling of the behavior of animals in order to understand the sensorimotor system, more specifically, a roundworm called C. elegans. The goal of my research is to understand the individual and collective behavior of this worm. If we can do this, these models will greatly accelerate our knowledge of the functional structure of the sensorimotor neural system.
Contrary to the common picture of a theorist, my approach to the problem is very "dirty". Most of my work involve collecting massive datasets of worm behavior and building statistical models from them. That's why I am also interested in various machine learning and statistical modelling techniques.
